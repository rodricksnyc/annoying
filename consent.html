Patient Preferences for Cancer Symptom Relief

Consent

The symptoms that people experience when they have cancer are an important component of their health. We are interested in your views about symptoms that may occur as a result of your cancer diagnosis or its treatment. We are inviting you to participate in a study that explores patients’ experiences with different symptoms in order to understand how symptom frequency, severity and interference affect how people feel and function.

Participation in this survey is voluntary. Your responses will be kept confidential and anonymous. You can read the information below and decide whether you wish to participate. If you agree to take part in this study, you will complete an online survey that will take approximately 15 minutes. The survey includes comparisons between pairs of people with different levels of symptoms. For these comparisons, you will provide your best guess as to which of the two people is better off. There are no &dquo;right” answers to these questions. Also in the survey will be demographic questions and questions about your own physical health and symptoms.

You will not personally benefit from being in this study, however, by taking part, you will provide knowledge that will be useful in better understanding the preferences of patients who have been treated for cancer. There are no expected significant risks associated with being in this study. To protect your confidentiality, we are not collecting any information that would identify you. Your individual answers will not be shared publicly. Group–level and summary results will be shared or published. As with any digital information, there is a slight chance that your data could be seen by people other than those on the research team. However, to protect you, we are not collecting any personally identifying information. All information you provide will be retained for 5 years.

Participants in this study will be offered a $5 Amazon gift card to thank them for their time. The researchers for this study will have no further contact with you after you finish answering the survey.

If, for any reason, you wish to stop providing answers to the questions on the survey, you may do so by closing your web browser. However, by consenting to be in this study you agree to allow us to use any data that you have provided up until that point.

This study is being conducted by the National Cancer Institute, in collaboration with the State Cancer Registries. It has been approved by the institutional review board of the National Institutes of Health. Please direct any inquiries you may have to Dr. Sandra Mitchell, at the National Cancer Institute (Sandra.mitchell@nih.gov or 240-276-6929)
